Sakura Online Ordering


An important aspect of online web applications is responsiveness to various platforms, accessibility to different users, and aesthetic quality. In this assignment, I chose a webpage to analyze, improve, and ultimately redesign according to the principles above.



problem

The online ordering page of Sakura, a Japanese restaurant in Providence, RI, is simple yet difficult to navigate. In terms of usability, the customer must navigate through several pages in order to view menu items, add them to their cart, and checkout. Because the color palette is mostly black and gray, the hierarchy of elements becomes difficult to discern and the customer loses sense of what is most important. Moreover, the lack of images and colors leads room for confusion as to what the customer is ordering or looking for, especially if they are unfamiliar with the names of food items.
❮
❯

improvements

My solution was to integrate more pops of color and create a clear hierarchy and sense of grouping between like elements. I placed a categorizer on the left hand side, which has drop down buttons that allow customers to quickly filter their options. Moreover, I incorporated images of each menu item and made a clear “Add to Cart” button, as well as the cart icon on the top right corner. Overall, this made the ordering website and process more succinct as customers could view the most important pieces of information all in one place.

redesign

Most importantly, my redesigned website is responsive to desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. As the size of the screen shrinks, the website adapts smoothly to the platform’s new dimensions, allowing users to seamlessly view the ordering page anywhere they go.
Redesigning the Sakura ordering page helped me to think through design principles of usability, memorability, efficiency, learnability, and the conceptual model. I learned how to make webpages respond to various screen dimensions, as well as which layouts were more common and fitting for desktop vs tablet vs mobile devices.
