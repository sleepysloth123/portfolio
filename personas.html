INTERFACES: BROWN UNIVERSITY WASHING MACHINES



Interfaces, defined as a point of interaction between a user and the object they are using, can be found everywhere – from soda dispensers to web browsers to laundry machines. To analyze what makes an ordinary interface successful or not, I had to step into the shoes of both a user and a UX designer. I observed users interacting with Brown University’s standard washing machines in order to gain a better understanding of how students interact with the machine interface, what users feel and think when interacting with the interface, and how the interface can be improved to enhance the user experiences.

pros and cons

Upon observing and interviewing various users interacting with the washing machines in the basement of my dorm, I came to the following observations:

  • Brown washing machines are one of the cheapest and most convenient ways to wash clothes for students
  • The machines are intuitive and simple to use
  • The holding capacity of each machine is unclear
  • The sticker labels on each machine is helpful, but not very explicit
  • Students must keep track of their own cycle times
  • Once the machine begins, there is no way to end the cycle prematurely
  • The machine does not seem to be wheelchair accessible

who uses these machines?

After gathering my data, I drew up two “personas”, or characters, that embodied a certain set of traits I observed in each user that interacted with the machine. Each persona is divided into Think, Feel, Say, and Do categories, which predict and summarize their state of being before, during, and after interaction with the machine.

The first persona is Forgetful Francis, who often forgets small details such as putting in soap before starting the washing machine and setting a timer to remind herself when the cycle ends.



The second persona is Hurried Harold, a student in a rush to prepare for his interview. As a result, he shows more carelessness and aggressiveness when using the laundry machines, highlighting some of the actions that the washing machines allow that may act as a source of distress for students.

storyboarding



Finally, I sketched out a storyboard of a beginning-to-end
interaction with the washing machine through the perspective of
someone like Hurried Harold.

Exploring interfaces and personas allows me to gain more insight into the user experience impacted by interface design. During this project, I learned how to analyze and summarize user experiences in order to realize potential issues and solutions to existing interfaces. Personifying these experiences serves as a valuable tool when designing my own interfaces, as it encourages me to consider multiple perspectives and audiences who each have different opinions and mannerisms that affect the way in which they interact with an interface.
